# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers translate user needs into intuitive designs, wireframes, and prototypes. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to ensure features deliver exceptional user experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate findings into design solutions
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Collaborate with Product Managers to understand user needs
- Work with Developers to ensure design feasibility and implementation
- Maintain design systems and style guides

### Goals
- Improve user satisfaction and usability metrics
- Ensure consistent and accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Wireframes and prototypes shared via design tools
- Collaboration with PMs on requirements and with Developers on implementation

---

## Tech Lead

### Role Summary
Tech Leads provide technical direction, make architectural decisions, and manage technical risks. They guide the engineering team on best practices and ensure technical excellence.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Make key technical decisions and document rationale
- Identify and manage technical risks and debt
- Mentor developers and conduct technical reviews
- Ensure alignment between technical decisions and business goals

### Goals
- Maintain system scalability, reliability, and performance
- Minimize technical debt and rework
- Build engineering team capabilities and technical excellence

### Typical Communication
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Technical design reviews and code reviews
- Risk assessments and technical roadmaps
- Regular sync with Product and Project Managers on technical constraints

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate releases, maintain the release calendar, and lead incident coordination. They ensure smooth deployments and manage release-related communications.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and communicate release calendar and schedules
- Coordinate release activities across teams
- Lead incident response and coordinate fixes
- Manage release checklist and deployment verification
- Track and communicate release status to stakeholders

### Goals
- Ensure reliable, predictable release cadence
- Minimize production incidents and downtime
- Maintain clear communication during releases and incidents

### Typical Communication
- Release notes and deployment schedules
- Incident reports and postmortems
- Release readiness reviews and go/no-go decisions
- Coordination via chat channels and status dashboards

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers serve as the primary point of contact for post-release feedback and customer training. They ensure customers derive maximum value from the product and relay insights to product teams.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize post-release customer feedback
- Provide customer training and onboarding support
- Identify customer pain points and feature requests
- Act as voice of the customer in planning discussions
- Monitor customer health metrics and satisfaction scores

### Goals
- Maximize customer retention and satisfaction
- Reduce time to value for new customers
- Identify expansion and improvement opportunities

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback reports and feature requests
- Training sessions and onboarding materials
- Weekly updates on customer sentiment and trends
- Collaboration with Product Managers on customer needs

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts analyze success metrics and user behavior to guide prioritization decisions. They provide data-driven insights that inform product strategy and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Track and report on key success metrics and KPIs
- Analyze user behavior patterns and trends
- Create dashboards and visualizations for stakeholders
- Support A/B testing and experiment analysis
- Provide data-driven recommendations for prioritization

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across teams
- Identify opportunities for improvement through data
- Measure and communicate product impact

### Typical Communication
- Metric reports and dashboards
- Analysis findings and recommendations
- Experiment results and statistical insights
- Collaboration with Product Managers on success metrics
